Fuel Range Calculator:

Driving "Pedal to the Metal"

This calculator uses the stoichiometric ratio for air to fuel in combination with the intake pressure findings cited, and given engine displacement to determine how much fuel is consumed per engine revolution. It then assumes a 1:1 gear ratio from the transmission, typical on the highest gear before overdrive, as well as a common 3.73 differential ratio, in combination with the given tire size to convert the result into distance(miles).
